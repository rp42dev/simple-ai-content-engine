## What is Professional Teeth Whitening?

Professional teeth whitening is a carefully supervised dental procedure performed by licensed practitioners. Unlike the whitening products you can buy in stores, professional whitening uses stronger, medical-grade bleaching agents—usually based on hydrogen peroxide—that safely penetrate the enamel to break down stains.

This treatment targets common tooth discolourations caused by drinks like coffee or tea, smoking, natural aging, and some medications. The goal is to remove these stains and lighten your teeth by several shades, creating a noticeably brighter smile.

Because it’s done under expert supervision, the procedure is customised to your tooth condition, ensuring optimal results with minimal risk.

## Types of Professional Teeth Whitening Treatments

### In-Clinic Whitening

In-office whitening is the fastest method. A high concentration bleaching gel is applied directly to your teeth, often with a protective barrier on your gums. To speed up the process, dentists commonly use a special light or laser that activates the gel.

This treatment usually takes 60 to 90 minutes and gives a visible difference immediately. It’s ideal if you want results in a single visit or ahead of an upcoming event.

### Take-Home Professional Kits

For a more gradual whitening process, your dentist can provide custom-fitted trays and professional-strength bleaching gel to use at home. These kits have lower concentration gel compared to in-office treatment but remain more effective than store-bought options.

You wear the trays for a set time each day, usually over one to two weeks, with your dentist’s guidance for safe and even results.

### Combination Treatments

Some patients benefit from a combined approach—starting with an in-clinic session to jump-start whitening, followed by take-home trays to maintain and extend the effect. This option balances speed and long-term maintenance.

## Who is a Good Candidate for Professional Teeth Whitening?

Most adults with healthy teeth and gums are suitable candidates, especially if their discoloration is due to external lifestyle factors like coffee or smoking.

However, whitening does *not* change the colour of crowns, veneers, or fillings — so extensive front tooth restorations may affect your outcome.

Pregnant or nursing women should delay whitening, and those with sensitive enamel or gum issues should consult a dentist first. At *from to* in Celbridge, we always perform a thorough evaluation before recommending whitening to ensure it’s right for you.

## What to Expect During a Teeth Whitening Appointment

Here’s how your in-office whitening session typically unfolds:

1. **Consultation & Exam:** Your dentist checks your oral health and discusses your shade goals.
2. **Teeth Cleaning:** A quick clean may be done to remove surface debris.
3. **Gum Protection:** A barrier or gel is applied to protect your gums.
4. **Application of Whitening Gel:** The bleaching agent is carefully applied to your teeth.
5. **Light Activation (if applicable):** A special light or laser enhances gel effectiveness.
6. **Treatment Duration:** You relax for 60–90 minutes while the gel works.
7. **Post-Treatment Instructions:** You'll receive advice to manage mild sensitivity or protect your results.

Some people may experience temporary tooth sensitivity, which usually eases within a day or two. The dental team will guide you on how to care for your teeth after treatment.

## Safety and Side Effects of Teeth Whitening

When performed professionally, teeth whitening is safe and well-tolerated. Common side effects include:

- **Temporary Tooth Sensitivity:** This typically lasts a short time and can be relieved with desensitizing toothpaste.
- **Mild Gum Irritation:** Avoiding contact of gels with soft tissues and following dentist instructions help prevent this.

Following your dentist’s guidance and not overusing whitening products are key to avoiding complications.

## Maintaining Your Whitened Teeth: Aftercare Tips

To keep your smile bright as long as possible:

- Limit intake of staining foods and drinks: coffee, tea, red wine, berries, and tobacco.
- Stick to regular oral hygiene: brush twice daily, floss, and consider whitening toothpaste.
- Use touch-up kits as recommended by your dentist.
- Attend your routine dental check-ups for professional cleaning and monitoring.
- Contact your dentist if sensitivity persists or other issues arise.
